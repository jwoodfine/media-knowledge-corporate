**Dual-Mandate Building Certifications** refers to the simultaneous delivery of two independently assessed building performance certifications for every Woodfine Building: verifiable Environmental Performance certification assessed under standards including BREEAM, and Human-Centric Wellness certification assessed under standards including WELL. Woodfine pursues both mandates concurrently because the institutional tenant base that anchors the portfolio — National Tenants undertaking Regional Market expansion — increasingly requires compliance with both certification types as a condition of occupancy, and legacy office inventory in Regional Markets typically cannot satisfy either.

## The Investable–Repurpose Split in Office Markets

The economic context for Dual-Mandate Building Certifications is the division of Regional Market office inventory into two categories based on capital market eligibility. Buildings that can demonstrate measurable environmental performance and human-centric workspace standards attract institutional capital and National Tenants expanding their Regional Market footprint. Buildings that cannot demonstrate these standards face constrained capital flows and a narrowing tenant base, as National Tenants exit the space in favour of inventory that meets their operational requirements.

Woodfine positions its portfolio to capture the tenant demand that is displaced when National Tenants conclude that legacy Regional Market office inventory no longer meets their occupancy standards. A National Tenant vacating a legacy building in a Regional Market requires a replacement space in the same market that satisfies its certification requirements. A Woodfine Building carrying Dual-Mandate certifications satisfies that requirement without requiring the tenant to relocate to a larger urban centre.

## Environmental Performance — BREEAM Standard

BREEAM (Building Research Establishment Environmental Assessment Method) assesses building performance across categories including energy use, water efficiency, materials sourcing, and site ecology. A BREEAM rating for a Woodfine Building reflects the building's measured performance against these criteria, independently assessed by a licensed BREEAM assessor.

The Geometry of Sustainability applied in Woodfine Building design — European Lighting Directive, Swiss Air Exchange Requirements, German Circulation Requirements — produces buildings whose structural characteristics align with BREEAM performance criteria. The 30% operating cost reduction that results from Geometry of Sustainability compliance is a consequence of building characteristics that also satisfy BREEAM energy and environmental performance thresholds.

## Human-Centric Wellness — WELL Standard

WELL (the WELL Building Standard) assesses building characteristics across domains including air quality, water quality, nourishment, light, movement, thermal comfort, sound, and mind. A WELL certification reflects a building's demonstrated performance across these domains, assessed against defined thresholds by a licensed WELL assessor.

The Human-Centric Wellness mandate captures the requirements of National Tenants whose professional services mandate — medical services, financial advisory, legal, academic — requires a workspace environment that demonstrably supports occupant health and cognitive performance. These tenants are the primary occupant category in Woodfine Buildings.

## Certification as a Competitive Positioning Tool

In Regional Markets where the supply of institutionally certified office space is limited, Dual-Mandate Building Certifications convert a building requirement that National Tenants set as a minimum threshold into a competitive differentiator at the property level. A National Tenant with a confirmed BREEAM and WELL requirement for its Regional Market locations has a constrained set of options in most Regional Markets — in many cases, a Woodfine Building under development is the only qualifying inventory entering the market at the time of the tenant's expansion decision.

This supply constraint is reinforced by the Geometry of Sustainability: achieving both BREEAM and WELL certification requires design characteristics that are fixed at the floor plate level and cannot be retrofitted into existing buildings without structural intervention. New construction is the only reliable path to simultaneous Dual-Mandate certification, which limits the competitive set to buildings built to those standards from the outset.
