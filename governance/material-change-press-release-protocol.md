A **material change** under NI 51-102 is a change in the business, operations, or capital
of the reporting issuer that would reasonably be expected to have a significant effect on
the market price or value of any of the issuer's securities.[^1] The material change
reporting protocol is event-driven and operates independently of the quarterly and annual
reporting cycle: when a material change occurs, the issuer must disclose it immediately.

## Definition and scope

The definition of material change encompasses both changes that have occurred and changes
that management has decided upon. A decision by a reporting issuer's management or board of
directors — even if not yet publicly known — constitutes a material change if it would
reasonably be expected to significantly affect the market price or value of the issuer's
securities.

Common categories of material change for real property holding companies and direct-hold
investment structures include:

- Completion or termination of a major acquisition or disposition
- Entry into or termination of a significant joint venture or partnership
- A change in capital structure (new debt, equity issuance, refinancing of material
  facilities)
- A significant change in the business model or strategic direction announced by the board
- A change in senior executive leadership with material implications for strategy
- The occurrence of a material adverse event affecting a significant portfolio asset

## Disclosure sequence

When a material change occurs or is decided upon, the disclosure sequence under NI 51-102
Part 7 is as follows:

**Step 1 — Immediate press release.** The issuer must immediately issue and file a press
release disclosing the nature and substance of the material change. "Immediately" is
interpreted in practice to mean within one business day of the change occurring or being
decided, subject to the confidentiality exception discussed below.

**Step 2 — Form 51-102F3 filing.** Within **10 calendar days** of the material change, the
issuer must file a Material Change Report on Form 51-102F3 with each securities regulatory
authority in the jurisdictions where the issuer is a reporting issuer.

## Content requirements for the press release

The material change press release must include:

- A description of the material change in terms sufficient for investors to assess its
  significance
- The date of the change
- Management's assessment of the impact on the issuer's operations or financial condition,
  to the extent reasonably practicable

## Forward-looking statements

Material change press releases frequently include forward-looking statements about the
expected impact of the change. Under NI 51-102 and the applicable safe harbour provisions,
forward-looking statements must:

- Be identified as forward-looking
- Identify the material factors that could cause actual results to differ materially from
  the forward-looking statement
- Include the material assumptions applied in making the forward-looking statement

Boilerplate cautionary language that does not specifically identify the material assumptions
and risk factors applicable to the particular forward-looking statement does not satisfy the
safe harbour requirements.[^2]

## Confidentiality exception

Where management determines that immediate disclosure of a material change would be
unduly detrimental to the issuer's interests — for example, where a transaction is at a
sensitive stage of negotiation and premature disclosure would jeopardise its completion —
NI 51-102 permits the issuer to maintain the material change in confidence temporarily.
The conditions for exercising this exception are stringent: the issuer must determine that
disclosure would be unduly detrimental, must file a confidential material change report with
the relevant securities regulators, and must update the assessment regularly until the
material change can be disclosed.
