**NI 51-102 Continuous Disclosure Obligations** is the National Instrument promulgated by
the Canadian Securities Administrators that establishes the ongoing disclosure requirements
applicable to reporting issuers in Canada.[^1] The annual reporting cycle under NI 51-102
requires a reporting issuer to file three primary disclosure documents within 90 days of its
fiscal year-end: audited annual financial statements prepared in accordance with IFRS as
adopted in Canada, an Annual Information Form (AIF, Form 51-102F2), and an annual
Management's Discussion and Analysis (MD&A).

## Annual financial statements

Annual financial statements filed under NI 51-102 must be prepared in accordance with IFRS
as adopted by the International Accounting Standards Board and must be audited by an
independent registered public accountant. The statements comprise:

- A statement of financial position (balance sheet) at fiscal year-end and at the prior
  fiscal year-end
- A statement of comprehensive income for the fiscal year and the comparative period
- A statement of changes in equity
- A statement of cash flows
- Notes to the financial statements, including significant accounting policies and judgements

For real estate investment entities and direct-hold operating companies, IFRS 16 (leases),
IAS 40 (investment property), and IFRS 13 (fair value measurement) are typically the
most materially significant standards. Fair value measurement of investment property under
IAS 40 requires either the fair value model (with changes through profit or loss) or the
cost model; the elected model must be applied consistently to the entire investment property
class.

## Annual Information Form — Form 51-102F2

The AIF provides a comprehensive description of the reporting issuer's business, risk factors,
capital structure, and governance. Required disclosure categories include:

- Business overview and corporate structure
- Risk factors material to the issuer's business
- Description of share capital and material contracts
- Directors and officers — names, municipalities of residence, and five-year biographical
  summaries
- Legal proceedings
- Auditors, transfer agents, and registrars

The AIF is a public document distributed through SEDAR+ (the System for Electronic Document
Analysis and Retrieval) and is the primary reference document through which institutional
investors and analysts assess a reporting issuer's structural characteristics.

## Annual Management's Discussion and Analysis

The annual MD&A is a narrative analysis by management of the reporting issuer's financial
condition and results of operations for the fiscal year, required to provide context and
analysis that the financial statements alone do not supply. The annual MD&A must address:

- Comparison of current-year results to the prior comparative year
- Analysis of liquidity and capital resources
- Discussion of material off-balance-sheet arrangements
- Analysis of contractual obligations
- Fourth-quarter disclosure (summarised or full, at the issuer's election)
- Discussion of critical accounting estimates and judgements
- Disclosure of any material changes in accounting policies

The MD&A may be filed as a standalone document or incorporated into an annual report to
shareholders, provided the SEDAR+ filing includes a clearly identified MD&A document meeting
all Form 51-102F1 requirements.[^2]

## Filing deadlines

Under NI 51-102, a reporting issuer that is a non-venture issuer must file its annual
financial statements, AIF, and annual MD&A within **90 calendar days** of the fiscal year-end.
A reporting issuer that is a venture issuer (listed on a venture exchange such as TSX Venture)
has **120 calendar days**.

All filings are made through SEDAR+ and are publicly accessible upon filing. The OSC and
other provincial securities regulators conduct annual review programs that assess compliance
with the technical requirements of NI 51-102 and the IFRS standards applied.
